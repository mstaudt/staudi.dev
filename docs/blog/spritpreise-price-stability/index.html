Recognize how stable a fuel price is

Posted on 22. July 2025

#Spritpreise

We’re all familiar with this moment: you discover an unbeatably low gas price, start the engine and set off. But when you arrive at the gas station, you’re disappointed: the price has just changed - upwards, of course.

To minimize these annoying surprises, we have upgraded our app with a new, crucial data point.

New on board: the average number of price changes per day Link to heading

From now on, you will find a new figure directly in the detailed view of your favorite petrol station, as shown in the picture: “About X price changes per day”.

What does this number tell you?

This small but powerful piece of information gives you crucial insights into the pricing strategy of a petrol station and helps you to better assess the stability of an offer:

  • A high number (e.g. 30-50 changes per day): This gas station acts very dynamically. It adjusts its prices extremely frequently and quickly to the market situation. If you see a good price here, you need to hurry. There is a high probability that the price will only be valid for a short period of time.
  • A low number (e.g. 5-10 changes per day): This gas station tends to be more consistent. The prices are much more stable. A good price that you see here is more likely to still be valid when you arrive. This gives you more planning security.

With this new information, you can now make a more informed decision whether it’s really worth going to a gas station a little further away. You no longer have to fly off blindly, but can factor price dynamics into your decision.

We believe that transparency is the best fuel for smart decisions.
